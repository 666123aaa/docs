To Be Seen As Her

This work situates "the platform image" as an apparatus of discipline: it promises visibility while formatting the body into legible gendered scripts. By combining collage, AI-generated imagery, and multi-channel montage, it exposes how intimacy and desire are governed as aesthetic labour.

The piece refuses narrative closure: repetition and rhythmic accumulation operate as a pressure system, where looking becomes both participation and constraint—an everyday training of the self under algorithmic attention.
