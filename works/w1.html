01 — Experimental video

To Be Seen As Her

This feminist experimental video combines social-media imagery, collage, and AI-generated visuals to examine power structures shaping female bodies, gender roles, and platformed visual culture. Staging a tension between predatory female animals and human female figures, it interrogates aesthetic discipline, the commodification of emotion, and the scripting of gendered identity, while holding space for plural, negotiated female subjectivities. Formally, it adopts a multi-channel logic within a single screen, layering perspectives and temporalities. Across four sections, the work moves through bodily alienation, performative identity, and speculative coexistence, culminating in a “declarative symbiosis structure”: an open-ended, collective system that refuses narrative closure. It asks how images can operate as interfaces for counter-discipline, reconfiguring rhythms of viewing and enabling feminist redefinition across time and form.

The piece refuses narrative closure: repetition and rhythmic accumulation operate as a pressure system, where looking becomes both participation and constraint—an everyday training of the self under algorithmic attention.
