01 — Experimental Video

To Be Seen As Her

A feminist experimental video combining social media imagery, collage, and AI-generated visuals. It examines power structures around female bodies, gender roles, and platform-based visual culture. Starting from a juxtaposition between predatory female animals and human female figures, the work interrogates aesthetic disciplining, emotional commodification, and the scripting of gendered identities.

The video often uses a multi-channel compositional logic, placing multiple perspectives and temporalities inside a single screen. Structured in distinct sections, it culminates in a “declarative symbiosis structure”: an open system that resists narrative closure and proposes images as interfaces for counter-discipline.

02 — Collaborative Game Art

Interactive Extension of the Video Work

A collaborative feminist experimental game art project critiquing the mechanisms of power embedded in female bodies, gender roles, and platform visual culture. The game uses satire and interaction to immerse the audience in the construction of female identity inside a digital environment, foregrounding how “gender labels” operate as disciplinary scripts.

03 — Experimental Film

Bloodline - Umbilical cord - Expectation

This experimental film examines how, within a family, “upper” positions transmit expectations downward through bloodline. A puppet family becomes the central metaphor: an orbital structure (earth–moon–satellite) visualizes hierarchical circulation. The “umbilical cord” signals kinship; colored threads representing expectation grow from the elder puppet’s heart, entangle the self, then pass through the middle generation to the child, who collapses under accumulated weight.

The ending—threads lighting up and rapidly extinguishing—stages the binding relation between bloodline → umbilical cord → expectation as an unbroken chain.

04 — Sound Work

Be quieter

A sound piece built from looping, randomized phrases of “expectation” that Chinese children repeatedly hear across life. The work treats repetition as both a cultural technology and an affective discipline—producing pressure through accumulation rather than explicit command.

05 — Interactive Installation

Family Expectations (Installation)

Colored threads wrap around three generations of body forms, visualizing “inheritance” as a transmission of weight, shape, and expectation rather than property. The interaction invites participants to write perceived parental expectations on colored sticky notes and attach them onto figures or threads; expectations not “assigned to the self” can be removed and placed in front. Weight (material) and language (context) co-produce the installation’s pressure field.

This documentation sits beside the film and sound work as the same thematic field—one motif, multiple media positions.
